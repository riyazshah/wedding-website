Accommodations

Seattle

There are pleny of wonderful neighborhoods all around Seattle to stay in, and both Airbnbs and Hotels are great options. Here are a few neighborhood recommendations:
  • Capitol Hill - want to be near all the bars and late night action? This is where you want to stay
  • Belltown - close to downtown, but a bit more vibrant with restaurants and bars
  • Downtown - most convenient location to touristy activities like Pike Place Market and museums
  • Fremont - more "neighborhood-y" with good eats. Well situated between downtown activities and Ballard, which is a great area to explore the local beer scene

Issaquah

Staying in Issaquah will be the most convenient to get to/from the venue. Even if you are staying in Seattle for the other parts of your trip, saying in Issaquah Friday/Saturday may be a good idea!

We are working on collecting info on potential room blocks and/or discount codes at these two hotels. We should have more details by March - check back then!
  • Hilton Garden Inn
  • Spring Hill Suites by Marriot

Getting There

  • For people coming in from out of town, if you want to do more hiking/exploring beyond the city, you may want to rent a car from the airport or make use of zipcar/turo while you are in the Seattle area for shorter trips
  • There is plenty of parking at the venue for those who are driving locally and/or renting cars
  • Uber/Lyft also pick up and drop off from the venue
  • The venue is unfortunately not accessible by public transit 😟
  • If you’re interested in catching a ride from the city with someone, let us know and we’ll do our best to organize some carpooling!

Multi-day trips

Come for a week or two and explore Washington!
National Parks
Rainier/Tahoma | Olympics | North Cascades
We live within a few hours of three amazing national parks! We love getting out and camping/hiking in them, and highly recommend making some time to explore if you can. Just be warned - campsite reservations go live 6 months in advance and book up quick! If you can be flexible, you can sometimes get lucky with mid-week campsites or last minute cancellations (in our experience).
San Juan Islands
Visit the San Juans
The San Juan Islands are a beautiful place to spend a few days away from the city, and are reachable by seaplane or ferry. Go for the hiking, camping, kayaking, sailing, whale watching, or just hanging out on rocky beaches and waving to Canada-ing. Say hi to the resident orca pod and see some stunning PNW sunsets.
Bellingham + Vancouver
Our local recommendations
Vancouver is just a 3hr drive up the coast from Seattle, with some amazing scenery along the way. (You can also take the train!! We’ve never done this, but it’s on the list!) It has some of the best dim sum we’ve ever had (don’t worry, here’s a map). Bellingham is a cute seaside city on the WA side of the border with a great beer and food scene that is also worth a stop. If you’re in Seattle and you’re wondering, “wouldn’t it be cool if the outdoors were EVEN CLOSER??” Bellingham might be right for you!
